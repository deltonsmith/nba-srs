PowerIndex Methodology

Updated daily during the NBA season.

Inputs

  • Games, scores, and dates from the Balldontlie API.
  • Player appearances and minutes from Balldontlie box scores.
  • Per-player values (v_p) derived from historical performance.

Data Inputs

  • Game outcomes (win/loss).
  • Margin of victory or defeat.
  • Recency weighting (recent games matter more).
  • Strength-of-opponent effect (beating strong teams counts more).

What PowerIndex Is

  • A daily-updated team-strength rating system.
  • Grounded in game results and how teams perform against opponents.
  • Comparable across all teams at a point in time (higher is better).
  • Built for consistency so trends are easy to track over the season.

What PowerIndex Is Not

  • Not a prediction guarantee.
  • Not betting advice.
  • Not an official NBA statistic.
  • Not a replacement for injury or news context.

Process

  • Compute lineup-adjusted margins per game using player values.
  • Iterate an SRS-style model to solve team ratings (mean-zero normalized).
  • Apply recency and opponent adjustments implicitly via game margins and home/away flags.

Update Frequency

  • Runs automatically on a schedule.
  • Typically updates after games are complete.
  • Timestamps are shown on the Rankings page as Last updated.

Outputs

  • Current rating for each team (higher is better, centered around 0).
  • Yesterday and last-week ranks when prior snapshots exist.
  • Daily JSON at data/ratings_current.json plus dated history snapshots.

Output Definitions

  • Rating: Current team strength (higher is better).
  • Δ (yesterday): Change vs. prior day rating when available.
  • LW (last week): Rating from one week ago when available.
  • Tier: Grouping based on rating relative to the field.
